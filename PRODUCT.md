# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Business owners, operations leads, and decision-makers at small-to-mid-size companies who are evaluating a software partner. They arrive frustrated by software that "nearly fits" — stretched spreadsheets, disconnected systems, off-the-shelf tools they have to bend their business around. They are not necessarily technical; they want to understand what is being built and trust the people building it. The primary job: decide whether Ace Digital Solutions is worth a consultation.

## Product Purpose

A marketing site for Ace Digital Solutions (ADS), a software company that builds custom software for businesses and develops its own products. The site exists to communicate competence and trustworthiness and to convert qualified visitors into booked consultations. Success = a visitor who feels "these people are serious and will understand my problem" and clicks "Book a consultation."

## Brand Personality

Classy, timeless, professional — Microsoft-like, user-first, never flashy or trendy. Confident without overselling; plain-spoken, no jargon, no buzzwords. The voice is the honest expert who tells you when you don't need custom software. Should evoke calm confidence and long-term reliability, not startup hype.

## Anti-references

- Trendy/flashy startup landing pages (gradient meshes, neon, glassmorphism).
- Anything that reads as AI-generated or template-shaped: tracked uppercase eyebrow over every section, identical icon+heading+text card grids, em-dash cadence, the modal "navy-on-white B2B SaaS" look.
- Editorial-magazine affectation (display-serif italic + drop caps + broadsheet grid) — wrong register for a software company.
- Overstated hero metrics and fake social proof.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Products and capability should be shown (real screenshots, concrete examples), not just described in cards.
2. **Earn trust through clarity.** Plain language and honest framing beat persuasion. If the simplest answer isn't custom software, say so.
3. **Distinct, not just restrained.** Restraint is the floor, not the goal — one committed signature move so the site reads as ADS specifically, not as a software-company template.
4. **Every element earns its place.** Devices like eyebrows, numbers, and cards are used only where they carry real meaning (a genuine sequence, a real grouping), never as default scaffolding.
5. **Built like the work.** The site should demonstrate the same craft and care ADS sells — fast, accessible, well-engineered.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body and placeholder text ≥4.5:1 contrast; visible focus indicators (already global); keyboard-operable nav and forms; honest form feedback (no false success states); `prefers-reduced-motion` honored for any motion added. Non-technical audience: no icon-only navigation, no unexplained jargon.
